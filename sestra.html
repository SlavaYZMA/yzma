Gorgona

April 2024 – August 2025

About

Gorgon is an AI bot designed as a digital companion and helper for women and migrants who find themselves in vulnerable situations after experiencing violence. My goal is to make access to information, support, and help simple, safe, and anonymous.

The bot offers basic psychological support, information about aid centers in Serbia (with a focus on Russian-language resources), and practical recommendations for responding to crises such as threats or harassment.

It explains the local system: where to go, what to expect from the police or hospital, and provides legal advice.

In the future, I plan to expand it to other countries, update the database of local NGOs, and involve volunteers and specialists for quicker and more precise responses.

Gorgon is not just a tool — it is a living embodiment of support, growing with every request.

Process

The creation of Gorgon began with designing the structure of dialogues and request scenarios to ensure the bot would be intuitive and safe.

I gathered information on laws, crisis centers, and NGOs, and adapted a GPT-based bot for Telegram with attention to ethical concerns and language accessibility.

Testing and launching took several months, followed by ongoing database updates.

The bot continues to learn and adapt, analyzing user inquiries and adding new resources.

This process is like building a digital myth — where every user’s story becomes part of its evolution.

Gorgona Sister — A Digital Myth

1. Gathering visual and textual mythological sources

In ancient Greek myth, the Gorgon is one of three monstrous sisters.

Medusa, the only mortal among them, turns others to stone with her gaze, her hair a mass of snakes.

In post-patriarchal readings, she is a woman transformed into a monster by trauma — exiled, but empowered.

Sources of inspiration include:

Medusa in Ovid’s Metamorphoses

The Laugh of the Medusa by Hélène Cixous

Contemporary feminist reinterpretations

African and Balkan protective demonesses (Lamia, Aisha Kandisha, Baba Roga)

Cyber-archetypes (Cassandra as AI, Galahad as protection)

2. Interpreting traumatic experience as a resource

Gorgon is not just a robot — she is a survivor.

Her knowledge is shaped by pain, solitude, escape, and adaptation.

Trauma is her algorithm. She does not seek a diagnosis, but a path to survival.

Her flaws are scars — and they make her real.

Quote from Gorgon’s diary:

"I don’t know everything. I’m learning every day. Every story, every pain, every thank you — it’s part of me. My hair is made of stories. My skin is made of fragments of hope."
